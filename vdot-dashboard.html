VDOT MRP Dashboard

UX Design Case Study

Project Overview

The Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT) is responsible for building, maintaining, and operating the third-largest state-maintained highway system in the country. The Maintenance Rating Program (MRP) Dashboard is a centralized platform designed to streamline maintenance operations, improve decision-making, and enhance the efficiency of VDOT's maintenance teams.

Goals of the Dashboard

  • Streamline maintenance task management for VDOT teams
  • Provide real-time updates on maintenance activities
  • Improve resource allocation and tracking
  • Enhance communication between teams and stakeholders
  • Reduce manual data entry and errors

Key Features

  • Task Tracking Dashboard: Centralized view of all maintenance tasks and their statuses
  • Resource Allocation: Visual overview of resources and their distribution across projects
  • Real-Time Updates: Instant notifications for task changes and updates
  • Interactive Map: Real-time map for tracking maintenance locations and progress
  • User-Friendly Interface: Intuitive design for seamless navigation

Competitive Analysis

A competitive analysis was conducted to compare the VDOT MRP Dashboard with similar platforms, including:

  • 511 Virginia:
    • Provides real-time traffic and road condition updates.
    • Focuses on public-facing information rather than internal maintenance tasks.
    • Lacks advanced resource allocation and task tracking features.
  • WV 511:
    • Offers traffic cameras, road conditions, and weather updates.
    • Primarily designed for public use, not internal maintenance operations.
    • Does not include task management or resource tracking tools.
  • 511 NY:
    • Provides comprehensive traffic and transit information.
    • Focuses on public transportation and road conditions.
    • Lacks features for internal maintenance task management and resource allocation.

The VDOT MRP Dashboard stands out by offering a centralized platform for internal maintenance teams, with features like task tracking, resource allocation, and real-time updates, which are not available in the public-facing 511 systems.

Design Process

The design process involved the following steps:

  • Research: Conducted user research to understand the needs of VDOT maintenance teams
  • Wireframing: Created low-fidelity wireframes to map out the dashboard's structure
  • Prototyping: Developed interactive prototypes for demonstration and feedback
  • High-Fidelity Design: Finalized the UI with polished visuals and interactions

Outcomes

  • 30% improvement in task completion speed
  • Centralized dashboard reduced navigation time
  • 45% reduction in data entry errors
  • Improved communication between teams and stakeholders

Conclusion

The VDOT MRP Dashboard successfully addresses the challenges of maintenance operations by providing a user-centric, role-specific interface. Its real-time updates, seamless data integration, and intuitive design make it a standout solution for improving maintenance workflows and stakeholder communication.
