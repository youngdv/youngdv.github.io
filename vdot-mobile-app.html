VDOT Maintenance Rating Program (MRP)

UI/UX Design Case Study

Project Overview

The Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT) is responsible for building, maintaining, and operating the third-largest state-maintained highway system in the country. VDOT's Maintenance Rating Program (MRP) is designed to provide vital information for making maintenance decisions based on facts and sound judgment.

The VDOT MRP App is a crowdsourcing tool developed to gather condition information of roadway assets from active VDOT maintenance personnel and Virginia roadway network users. The app aims to streamline data collection, improve decision-making, and enhance the overall efficiency of VDOT's maintenance operations.

Goals of the App

  • Streamline task management for VDOT inspectors
  • Reduce manual data entry and errors
  • Provide real-time updates on maintenance tasks
  • Improve resource allocation and tracking
  • Enhance overall operational efficiency

Key Features

  • Task Tracking Dashboard: Centralized view of all tasks and their statuses
  • Resource Allocation: Visual overview of resources and their distribution
  • Real-Time Updates: Instant notifications for task changes and updates
  • Interactive Map: Real-time map for tracking maintenance locations and progress
  • User-Friendly Interface: Intuitive design for seamless navigation

UI Design Process

The design process involved the following steps:

  • Wireframing: Created low-fidelity wireframes to map out the app's structure
  • Prototyping: Developed interactive prototypes for demonstration and feedback
  • High-Fidelity Design: Finalized the UI with polished visuals and interactions

Outcomes

Conclusion

The redesigned interface significantly improved operational efficiency through streamlined workflows and enhanced visibility of critical maintenance data. The VDOT MRP App has become an essential tool for VDOT personnel, enabling them to make data-driven decisions and maintain Virginia's highway system more effectively.
